Create New Account

To create a new account, you have to...

  1. Choose the chain, practice on Jungle3 or use the real EOS chain
  2. Pick a memorable account name, 12 characters, range: a-z or 1-5
  3. Who do you trust? Enter the custodian account name, 12 characters
  4. Create a new key using biometrics on your phone
  5. Send it to your trusted friend, they will create the account for you

Get yourself an account!

  1. Choose the blockchain (EOS/Jungle3)
  2. Choose your account name, the app will check if it is available (legal 12 character names only)
  3. Create a key pair using biometrics for it, the magic happens here!
  4. Who do you want to trust to be the admin for your account? The Owner permission in EOS terminology. In the future this will be more complicated, you'll be able to choose a threshold and some accounts, keys and time delays, or some well known accounts with a combination of keys and time delays in them
  5. Send the account name and the public key to a friend who will create the account for you

Want to help a friend create an account?

  1. Choose a blockchain (currently only jungle3 or EOS)
  2. Explain to them about regular account name format - 12 characters of the range: a-z or 1-5
  3. Who will be the Owner permission for their account? Explain to them they need to trust you if they choose you to be the Owner, this will be used if their phone is lost or broken
  4. Send them a link from this app which will get them to this app on their phone and will pre-populate the fields for them
  5. They will only need to create a key pair using biometrics and choose tehir account name, they will send back to you, you will use your wallet to create the account for them
  6. You will pay for their RAM
  7. You can also send them some EOS and let them know about powerup

Backup EOS in a Box data

EOS in a Box does not manage your data for you. All your data is on your phone's local storage. If you want, you can export all data as a json object and save it in any place you like, perhaps send it to yourself in an email. In the future this might change. In any case, your private keys are never exposed - they can't, since you're using your phone's hardware security module to generate and manage keys for you. This backup only has the public keys. This is also why you need to use the custodian account to replace keys if you switch phones.

TL;DR

Too Long; Didn't Read

  1. A simple front end web wallet for the EOS blockchain
  2. Blockchain uses a system of key pairs
  3. Private keys should be kept private, you use them to send transactions from your account to the blockchain
  4. Public Keys are public, they are related to the private keys and allow the blockchain to verifie your transactions are valid
  5. EOS blockchain has a permission system with an everyday key, called "Active" and a master key called "Owner"
  6. EOS in a Box creates accounts on the EOS blockchain with an easy and secure "Active" key leveraging your phone's built-in hardware security module with Face-ID or fingerprints
  7. EOS in a Box requires a "Custodian" account in case your phone gets stolen or breaks
  8. Think of the "Active" key as the key to your house
  9. Think of the "Owner" key as a Locksmith, someone that can break into your house but is trustworthy
  10. You can be your own Custodian (future) by securly managing a key pair and creating an EOS in a Box account with the Public Key as the Custodian
  11. Don't put large amounts of tokens in this wallet if you don't understand the implications
